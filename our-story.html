Our Story

Built slowly. Built deliberately.

Evergrain began as a side project in a one-car garage in Eagle Rock — a borrowed DSLR, a printer that jammed every third photo, and one belief: the photo booth should be the most beautiful object in the room.

Who we are

A small studio. A specific obsession.

We're a husband-and-wife team — Mariko, a former wedding photographer, and Dean, a fabrication nerd who builds furniture on weekends. Evergrain sits at the intersection of those two things: real cameras and real craftsmanship.

We don't sell the same booth as everyone else because we didn't buy ours off a parts list. We designed our enclosure, sourced our lights, dialed in our print profiles, and we still set up every event ourselves.

The journey

Five years, one quiet idea.

2019

The garage.

A friend asked us to bring a "photo thing" to her wedding. We rigged a DSLR to a laptop, taped a curtain to the wall, and printed 80 strips on a borrowed dye-sub. Eight people emailed the next week asking who built it.

2021

The booth, version one.

Dean built our first proper enclosure out of walnut and linen — modular, transportable, and quiet. We did 30 events that year. We learned what lighting actually mattered.

2023

The shift to DSLR-first.

We retired the iPad-based shooter for good. Every photo from this point forward is a real 24-megapixel image, lit with a softbox and a fill, and printed on archival photo stock.

Today

A two-booth studio.

We can take two events on a weekend. We still set up every one ourselves. We turn down a lot of work that doesn't feel like a fit, and we're at peace with that.

What guides us

Three principles, no exceptions.

Real cameras.

Phone cameras and tablets are fine for snapshots. We brought a DSLR because we wanted images that hold up at 8 × 10.

Considered design.

The booth should look at home next to your florals and signage, not be hidden in a corner.

Real prints.

Dye-sublimation, archival, instant-dry. Hand them out the same night and they'll still look right in five years.

A note

"We don't want to be the biggest photobooth company in California. We want to be the one your planner quietly recommends because she trusts it'll go perfectly."

Mariko & Dean — Founders
